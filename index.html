About Us

At Badekaka, our mission is to revolutionize agriculture by fostering strong partnerships between farmers and investors. We are committed to empowering farmers with the resources and support they need to thrive, while providing investors with rewarding opportunities to contribute to sustainable food production and rural development.

Badekaka is an innovative agricultural investment platform that connects farmers with investors through a produce-backed farming model. Our mission is to provide farmers with the resources they need to expand production while offering investors an opportunity to participate in agriculture and benefit from the value created during harvest.

For more than three years, Badekaka has successfully supported farmers and profited investors through a transparent and sustainable agricultural partnership system.

Our Story

Badekaka was founded on a simple idea: many hardworking farmers possess fertile lands, farming knowledge, and the willingness to work, but lack the sufficient financial resources are needed to maximize production.

Badekaka was born out of a passion for agriculture and a desire to create a more equitable and sustainable food system. We recognized the challenges faced by farmers in accessing capital and resources, and the untapped potential for investors to make a positive impact.

Our platform connects these two groups, enabling them to work together towards a common goal of growing prosperity through agriculture.

How It Works

From seed to harvest, every step is designed for shared success.

01

Farmer Requests Agricultural Support

A farmer applies for support to cultivate crops such as rice and submits details of the farming project.

02

Investor Provides Funding

Investors fund the equivalent value of agricultural produce before the planting season.

  • One bag of rice is valued at ₦30,000
  • A farmer requires support equivalent to 5 bags of rice
  • The investor provides ₦150,000 for farm operations
03

Cultivation and Monitoring

The farmer uses the funds for land preparation, seeds, fertilizers, labor, and farm management throughout the season.

04

Harvest and Produce Repayment

At harvest, the farmer returns the agreed quantity of produce to the investor.

  • Farmer receives ₦150,000 in funding
  • Farmer agrees to return 5 bags of rice after harvest
  • Farmer retains the remaining harvest for personal profit and business growth
